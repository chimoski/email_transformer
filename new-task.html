Greyhaven Q&A
with Tyler Deeb

We talk a lot about what things smell like. But Greyhaven started as a place in my head ... quiet, wind-worn, and a little haunting. If you're interested in how that became a fragrance, I answered a few questions below.

Explore Greyhaven
Explore Greyhaven
Q.
What inspired Greyhaven?
A.
I kept picturing this place I’d never been, cold and quiet, near the sea, where moss climbs everything and the wind leans the trees. A small coastal town, slow but full of life. Salt in the air, woodsmoke, open windows. Peaceful, clear, a little wild. That image became a scent. That scent became Greyhaven.
 
Q.   So what does it smell like?

A.   Salt. Pine. Moss. If you’ve ever walked through a coastal forest just after it rained, it’s like that. Wet bark, herbal air, distant waves. Not clean. Not spicy. Just... alive.
 
Q.
What makes it different from your other fragrances?
A.
Apart from being universally loved? It’s the one that feels most transportive. Most cinematic. I don’t even wear it every day, but when I do, it makes me feel grounded and a little mysterious.
 
Q.   Is it more for men or women?

A.   Both. We didn’t design Greyhaven to remind you of a person, but instead a place. Somewhere you want to remember, be, and share with other people.
 
Q.
Do you think you’ll ever make a scent as captivating as Greyhaven again?
A.
I think so. We have two new fragrances coming out soon with one in particular I think will be a fan favorite. That said, there’s something about Greyhaven, like it already existed in your mind and I just uncovered it. Those moments are hard to come by.
